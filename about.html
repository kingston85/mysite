About Me

Professional Biography

I am a results-driven Environmental Science and Engineering professional with a passion for sustainable development and environmental stewardship. With a Master's degree from the University of Science and Technology Beijing and a Bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering from Tianjin University, I bring a strong technical foundation to my work in academia and public service.

Currently, I serve as a Lecturer of Sustainable Development and Environmental Soil Chemistry at the University of Liberia, where I inspire the next generation of environmental scientists. Simultaneously, I contribute to Liberia's environmental protection efforts as a Chemical Remediation Analyst in the ERRS Department at the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).

My research interests center around carbon emission reduction, sustainable waste management, and environmental remediation. I am particularly interested in applying life cycle assessment approaches to develop practical solutions for environmental challenges in the West African context.

Beyond my professional life, I am a proud father to my 15-year-old son, Augustine. My Christian faith informs my commitment to environmental stewardship, viewing it as both a scientific endeavor and a moral responsibility.
