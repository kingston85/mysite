Faith & Reflections

Exploring the intersection of Christian faith, environmental stewardship, and professional work

My Faith Journey

My Christian faith forms the foundation of my identity and provides the moral compass that guides both my personal and professional life. As a believer, I view environmental stewardship not merely as a professional obligation but as a sacred responsibility to care for God's creation.

Growing up in Liberia, I witnessed firsthand both the beauty of our natural environment and the challenges that arise when we fail to properly steward these resources. My journey of faith has helped me understand that protecting our environment is both spiritually fulfilling and vital for the wellbeing of communities.

As a father to my son Augustine, I strive to model values of responsibility, care, and respect for God's creation. I believe that intergenerational equity—ensuring that future generations inherit a healthy planet—is both an environmental principle and a Christian value.

On this page, I share reflections on how my faith informs my approach to environmental science, teaching, and public service. These personal thoughts represent my ongoing journey to integrate spiritual values with professional practice.

Guiding Scriptures

Biblical passages that inspire my approach to environmental stewardship

"The Lord God took the man and put him in the Garden of Eden to work it and take care of it."

Genesis 2:15

This passage establishes humanity's original mandate to care for creation. The Hebrew words for "work" and "take care" imply both cultivation and protection. As environmental professionals, we continue this sacred task of responsible stewardship, finding ways to balance human needs with environmental preservation.

"The earth is the Lord's, and everything in it, the world, and all who live in it; for he founded it on the seas and established it on the waters."

Psalm 24:1-2

This psalm reminds us that the earth ultimately belongs to God, not to us. This perspective shapes my approach to environmental management—we are caretakers, not owners. This calls for humility in our interactions with the natural world and recognition that we are accountable for how we treat what has been entrusted to us.

"The creation waits in eager expectation for the children of God to be revealed. For the creation was subjected to frustration, not by its own choice, but by the will of the one who subjected it, in hope that the creation itself will be liberated from its bondage to decay and brought into the freedom and glory of the children of God."

Romans 8:19-21

Paul's profound insight connects human redemption with creation's restoration. This passage inspires me to see environmental work as participation in God's redemptive plan for all creation. When we work to restore damaged ecosystems or prevent pollution, we participate in this broader hope for creation's renewal.

Faith-Based Environmental Principles

Core values that guide my approach to environmental stewardship

Stewardship

Recognizing that the earth's resources are entrusted to our care by God, requiring responsible management and conservation for future generations.

Justice

Ensuring that environmental benefits and burdens are distributed fairly, with special concern for the poor and vulnerable who often bear the greatest impacts of environmental degradation.

Sustainability

Developing practices and systems that meet present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

Community

Recognizing that environmental challenges require collective action and shared responsibility, informed by the Christian call to love our neighbors.

Hope

Approaching environmental challenges with optimism grounded in faith, believing that positive change is possible through dedicated action and divine guidance.

Compassion

Extending care and concern to all living beings as part of God's creation, recognizing the intrinsic value of diverse ecosystems and species.

Recommended Readings

Books that have influenced my thinking on faith and environmental stewardship

Caring for Creation: The Evangelical's Guide to Climate Change and a Healthy Environment

By Mitch Hescox and Paul Douglas

This book provides a balanced perspective on climate change from an evangelical Christian viewpoint, emphasizing the biblical mandate to care for creation while addressing common misconceptions.

For the Beauty of the Earth: A Christian Vision for Creation Care

By Steven Bouma-Prediger

A theological exploration of environmental stewardship that grounds creation care in biblical principles and Christian tradition, offering practical guidance for living more sustainably.

Laudato Si': On Care for Our Common Home

By Pope Francis

Though from a Catholic perspective, this encyclical offers insights valuable across Christian traditions, addressing the interconnection between environmental degradation, poverty, and human dignity.
