Master's Thesis Research

Research on Construction and Demolition Waste Recycling in Beijing Based on a Life Cycle Assessment Approach

My master's thesis focused on developing sustainable strategies for managing construction and demolition waste in urban environments, using Beijing as a case study. Through life cycle assessment methodology, I evaluated the environmental impacts of various waste management scenarios and identified optimization opportunities.

The research involved collecting data from construction sites, analyzing waste composition, and modeling different recycling approaches to determine the most environmentally beneficial pathways. The findings provide valuable insights for urban planners and waste management authorities in developing efficient and sustainable waste management systems.

Estimation and Projection of Construction and Demolition Waste Generation in Beijing Using Empirical Construction Data

Emmanuel, S. & Lei, Z.

Journal of Waste Management & Research, 2023

Rapid urban growth generates unmanageable volumes of construction and demolition (CDW) waste in cities worldwide, necessitating reliable quantification of current and future waste trends to inform policy. Using empirical data and a straightforward methodology suited to data-scarce regions, this study estimated Beijingâ€™s CDW output from 2008 to 2022. CDW generation rates reflecting material losses and demolition processes were multiplied by construction/demolition areas to quantify waste. Results reveal over a 4-fold increase in CDW from 31 million tons in 2008 to 52 million tons in 2022. Concrete, brick and mortar comprised 85% of waste composition. Construction waste accounted for a larger proportion recently, totaling 57% over the 15-year analysis period. Using a -1% construction area growth rate and 40% project completion rate, CDW generation was forecast to reach 55 million tons by 2033. The exponential rise, outpacing utilization capacity, underlines the urgent need for CDW minimization, recycling and sustainable management aligned with circular economy principles in Beijing and similarly developing cities. Although omitted excavation waste contributes uncertainty, the scalable quantification methodology could help developing cities plan waste facilities and policies. While projected CDW growth rates may primarily reflect wider construction sector changes rather than climate policies, lack of linkage with economy-wide decarbonization trajectories highlights an area needing more research. By generating new insights plus an adaptable city-level waste modeling approach, this exploratory study provides a foundation for subsequent work to track on-site audits, connect localized data to carbon budgets, and realize ambitious reuse targets.

Current Research Projects

Ongoing investigations and collaborative research initiatives

Cost-Effective Soil Remediation Techniques for Liberia

In Progress

This research project aims to identify and adapt soil remediation techniques that are both environmentally effective and economically feasible for implementation in Liberia. The study focuses particularly on contamination from petroleum products and heavy metals in both urban and rural settings.

Collaborators: EPA Liberia, University of Liberia

Timeline: 2024-2026

Carbon Footprint Analysis of Monrovia's Urban Transport System

Planning Stage

This project will assess the carbon emissions associated with Monrovia's transportation infrastructure and identify potential interventions for reducing the city's carbon footprint. The research will include traffic surveys, fuel consumption analysis, and modeling of alternative transportation scenarios.

Collaborators: Ministry of Transport, University of Liberia

Timeline: 2025-2027

Integrated Waste Management Framework for Liberian Municipalities

Data Collection

This project aims to develop a comprehensive waste management framework that municipalities in Liberia can adapt to their specific contexts. The research includes waste characterization studies, assessment of current disposal practices, and development of intervention strategies.

Collaborators: Monrovia City Corporation, EPA Liberia

Timeline: 2024-2025
