EPA Liberia Work

Contributing to environmental protection and chemical remediation in Liberia

My Role as Chemical Remediation Analyst

As a Chemical Remediation Analyst in the Environmental Research and Radiation Safety (ERRS) Department at the Environmental Protection Agency of Liberia, I contribute to national efforts to address chemical contamination and hazardous substances that pose risks to human health and the environment.

My work involves identifying, assessing, and developing remediation strategies for sites contaminated with petroleum products, heavy metals, pesticides, and other hazardous chemicals. I conduct field investigations, analyze environmental samples, and recommend appropriate cleanup methodologies based on scientific principles and regulatory requirements.

This role allows me to apply my academic knowledge in environmental science and chemical engineering to real-world environmental challenges. I work closely with interdisciplinary teams, local communities, and government stakeholders to implement effective solutions that protect public health and restore environmental quality.

My position bridges scientific expertise with practical application, allowing me to contribute directly to Liberia's environmental protection efforts while also informing my research and teaching with relevant, real-world experience.

The ERRS Department

Understanding the critical role of Environmental Research and Radiation Safety at EPA Liberia

About the Department

The Environmental Research and Radiation Safety (ERRS) Department is a specialized unit within the Environmental Protection Agency of Liberia that focuses on addressing environmental emergencies and implementing remediation actions for contaminated sites. The department plays a crucial role in protecting public health and the environment from acute and chronic hazards associated with chemical releases and pollution incidents.

ERRS serves as the primary responder to environmental emergencies such as oil spills, chemical releases, and hazardous material incidents throughout Liberia. The department also manages long-term remediation projects for contaminated sites, working to restore environmental quality and reduce risks to nearby communities.

Key Responsibilities

Emergency Response

Responding to environmental emergencies and containing hazardous material releases

Site Assessment

Evaluating contaminated sites to determine the extent and nature of pollution

Remediation Planning

Developing cleanup strategies based on site characteristics and contamination type

Cleanup Oversight

Managing remediation projects and ensuring compliance with environmental standards

Community Engagement

Informing and involving local communities in the remediation process

Monitoring & Evaluation

Assessing the effectiveness of remediation efforts over time

Key Projects

Significant remediation initiatives and environmental response work

Petroleum Contamination Remediation at Old Port Facility

Monrovia Harbor Area

This project addresses historical petroleum contamination at a former port facility that operated without adequate environmental controls. Soil and groundwater assessments revealed significant hydrocarbon contamination that poses risks to nearby communities and marine environments.

Current Activities:

  • Implementation of bioremediation techniques for contaminated soil
  • Installation of groundwater monitoring wells to track contamination plume
  • Application of oxygen-releasing compounds to enhance natural attenuation
  • Community education program about site risks and remediation process

My Role:

  • Leading site characterization and contaminant delineation
  • Designing bioremediation approach based on site-specific conditions
  • Conducting regular monitoring of remediation effectiveness
  • Coordinating with laboratory for analysis of environmental samples

Heavy Metal Assessment and Remediation Program

Former Mining Areas, Bomi County

This comprehensive project addresses heavy metal contamination in soil and water sources near abandoned mining operations. Initial assessments identified elevated levels of lead, arsenic, and mercury that pose significant health risks to surrounding communities who use local water sources and grow crops in the affected areas.

Current Activities:

  • Detailed mapping of contamination extent through systematic soil and water sampling
  • Implementation of soil stabilization techniques to reduce metal mobility
  • Installation of filtration systems for community water supplies
  • Development of safe agricultural practices for affected areas

My Role:

  • Designing sampling protocols for heavy metal assessment
  • Evaluating remediation options based on contamination profiles
  • Coordinating with international experts on metal stabilization techniques
  • Leading community education on exposure reduction measures
