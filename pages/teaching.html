Academic Teaching

Educating the next generation of environmental scientists and sustainable development professionals.

Teaching Philosophy

My approach to teaching bridges theory and practice, empowering students to become change agents in their communities. I create an inclusive learning environment where students engage with complex environmental challenges through critical thinking and hands‑on experiences.

I emphasize the interdisciplinary nature of environmental science, helping students understand the interconnections between scientific, social, economic and ethical dimensions of environmental issues.

Current Courses

Environmental Soil Chemistry

ENV 305

This course examines the chemical processes occurring in soils and their influence on plant growth, water quality, nutrient cycling and contaminant behaviour.

Sustainable Water Resource Management

ENV 310

Explore principles of water resource management and the impact of climate change on hydrological systems, with emphasis on sustainable solutions for Liberia.

Environmental Impact Assessment

ENV 220

Students learn to evaluate environmental impacts of proposed projects, interpret regulatory frameworks and develop mitigation strategies.
