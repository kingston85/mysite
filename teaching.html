Teaching

Inspiring and equipping the next generation of environmental scientists and sustainability leaders

My Teaching Philosophy

As an educator in environmental science, I believe in creating an inclusive learning environment that balances rigorous academic content with practical real-world applications. My teaching approach is guided by three core principles:

"Environmental education must transcend theoretical concepts to inspire action and cultivate stewards of our planet's future."

Contextualized Learning: I emphasize the importance of framing environmental challenges within local contexts. For my students at the University of Liberia, this means connecting global environmental concepts to West African realities, making the content immediately relevant to their lived experiences.

Experiential Education: My courses incorporate field work, laboratory analysis, and case studies that allow students to apply theoretical knowledge to practical situations. This hands-on approach cultivates critical thinking and problem-solving skills essential for environmental professionals.

Inclusive Mentorship: I strive to create a supportive atmosphere where students feel empowered to explore their interests within environmental science. By recognizing diverse learning styles and cultural perspectives, I help each student discover their unique path to contributing to environmental solutions.

Current Courses

Specialized courses I teach at the University of Liberia

Environmental Soil Chemistry

ENV-305

Advanced

This course explores the chemical processes occurring in soils and their impact on environmental quality, contaminant behavior, and remediation strategies. Special emphasis is placed on soil conditions in tropical environments.

Key Topics

  • Soil composition and formation processes
  • Adsorption phenomena and ion exchange
  • Organic matter dynamics in tropical soils
  • Heavy metal contamination and mobility
  • Remediation approaches for contaminated soils
Monday & Wednesday, 10:00 AM - 11:30 AM

Waste Management & Recycling

ENV-412

  • Case studies from West African cities

Environmental Impact Assessment

ENV-440

Teaching Approaches

Innovative methods I employ to enhance student learning and engagement

Laboratory Investigations

Students engage in hands-on laboratory work to analyze soil samples, test water quality, and investigate contamination levels. These practical sessions reinforce theoretical concepts and develop essential analytical skills.

Field Excursions

Regular field trips to environmental sites across Liberia, including watersheds, waste management facilities, and remediation projects, providing students with direct exposure to real-world environmental challenges.

Collaborative Projects

Small group research projects that address local environmental issues, encouraging teamwork, leadership, and the application of multidisciplinary approaches to complex problems.

Case-Based Learning

Analysis of environmental case studies from Liberia and around the world, challenging students to apply theoretical knowledge to practical situations and develop critical thinking skills.

Community Engagement

Service-learning opportunities that connect students with local communities facing environmental challenges, fostering civic responsibility and providing valuable experience in stakeholder communication.

Technology Integration

Incorporation of digital tools, environmental modeling software, and geographic information systems to enhance students' technical literacy and analytical capabilities.
